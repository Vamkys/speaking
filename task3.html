1. Sightseeing in Russia

1. What region of Russia do you live in? What is it famous for?
Actually, I live in the Oryol region. It is famous for its beautiful landscapes and its connection to great Russian writers like Turgenev.
2. What are the most popular tourist destinations in your country?
The most popular places are definitely Moscow and Saint Petersburg because of their history. However, Lake Baikal and the Altai Mountains are also becoming very trendy for nature lovers.
3. What historical landmarks in your city or region would you recommend visiting?
I’d recommend visiting the local parks and museums dedicated to our history. They offer a great atmosphere where you can learn about how people lived in the past.
4. Why do you think some people prefer travelling abroad rather than within Russia?
I suppose they want to experience different cultures and climates. Also, sometimes flights to Europe or Asia can be cheaper than domestic flights to distant parts of Russia like Kamchatka.
5. What would you say to a friend who is planning a trip to Russia for the first time?
I would advise them to prepare for the weather and learn some basic Russian phrases. Also, I’d suggest visiting both big cities and small traditional towns to see the real Russia.

2. Famous People of Russia

1. Who is the most famous person from your home region?
As I’ve mentioned, my region is linked to Ivan Turgenev. He was a world-famous writer whose books are still studied in schools today.
2. In your opinion, which Russian scientist or inventor has changed the world?
I believe it’s Dmitry Mendeleev. His Periodic Table is the foundation of modern chemistry, and without it, many scientific discoveries wouldn't have happened.
3. What Russian athletes or sports figures are you proud of?
I’m proud of our figure skaters because they always show incredible results. Their discipline and hard work are a great example for young people.
4. Do you think it is easy to be a famous person nowadays? Why?
I don't think so, because famous people are always under pressure from social media. Every mistake they make is discussed by everyone, so it must be very stressful.
5. What field would you like to become famous in, and why?
To be honest, I haven't decided yet, but I'm interested in linguistics and education. I’d like to be known for helping people learn languages effectively.

3. Russian Holidays and Traditions

1. What is the most important holiday for your family?
The most important one is New Year’s Eve. We always gather together, prepare special food, and exchange gifts at midnight.
2. How do people usually celebrate Maslenitsa in Russia?
People usually cook pancakes, which symbolize the sun, and take part in outdoor activities. At the end of the week, they often burn a straw effigy to say goodbye to winter.
3. What traditional Russian food do you usually have during holidays?
We often have salads like "Olivier" or "Shuba." Also, my family likes traditional pies with different fillings like cabbage or meat.
4. Why do you think it’s important to remember your roots and traditions?
It’s vital because traditions give us a sense of belonging. They connect us to our ancestors and help keep our unique culture alive in a globalized world.
5. If you could create a new public holiday, what would it be?
I would create a "Psychology Day" where people could focus on their mental health. It would be a day to rest, reflect, and learn how to manage stress.

4. Nature and Environmental Issues

1. What is the climate like in the part of Russia where you live?
The climate in my region is temperate continental. We have quite cold winters with snow and warm, sometimes even hot, summers.
2. Are there any environmental problems in your home town?
Yes, the main issues are air pollution from cars and a lack of proper recycling systems. We also have problems with littering in public parks.
3. What do you and your family do to protect the environment?
We try to save water and electricity at home. Also, I always take a reusable bag when I go shopping to reduce the amount of plastic waste.
4. Do you think schools should have more lessons about ecology? Why?
Definitely, because children need to understand how their actions affect the planet. If we learn about ecology early, we will grow up to be more responsible citizens.
5. Is it popular among your friends to live an "eco-friendly" lifestyle?
It’s becoming more popular. Some of my friends have started sorting their trash, and many prefer eco-friendly brands of clothes or cosmetics.

5. Life in the City and Countryside

1. Where would you prefer to live: in a big city or in a small village?
I’d prefer to live in a big city. I like the fast pace of life, the variety of shops, and the fact that everything I need is always close by.
2. What are the main disadvantages of living in a metropolis?
The biggest downsides are traffic jams and air pollution. Also, it can be very noisy and crowded, which sometimes makes people feel stressed.
3. Why do many young people move from the countryside to the city?
I believe they move for better education and job opportunities. Big cities offer more career paths and a more active social life for teenagers.
4. What are the benefits of spending holidays in the countryside?
The main benefit is the fresh air and the chance to relax in nature. It’s a great way to take a break from gadgets and the noise of the city.
5. How has city life changed in Russia over the last 10 years?
I think it has become much more digital. We now have delivery services for everything and better public transport systems with electronic payments.

6. Science and Space Exploration

1. What do you know about the first man in space?
The first man in space was Yuri Gagarin. He made his famous flight in 1961, which was a huge breakthrough for all of humanity.
2. Are you interested in news about space exploration? Why?
Yes, I find it fascinating because space is still a big mystery. I like reading about the possibility of living on other planets in the future.
3. What Russian scientific discovery do you find the most useful?
I think the invention of the radio by Alexander Popov was crucial. It paved the way for modern communication, including television and mobile phones.
4. Why do you think the government spends so much money on space programs?
It’s because space research leads to new technologies that we use in everyday life, like satellite navigation. It’s also important for national prestige.
5. Would you like to go to space as a tourist one day?
To be honest, I’m not sure because it seems quite dangerous. However, if the technology becomes safer, it would be an incredible experience to see the Earth from above.

7. Schooling in Russia

1. What is your favourite subject at school and why?
My favourite subjects are English and Social Studies. I enjoy learning languages because they open up new worlds, and Social Studies helps me understand how society works.
2. What do you usually do during your school breaks?
I usually chat with my friends or check my phone. Sometimes I use this time to prepare for the next lesson or have a quick snack.
3. Do you think the Russian school system prepares students for real life?
In some ways, yes, because it teaches us discipline and basic knowledge. However, I think we need more practical lessons, like how to manage money or taxes.
4. What is your opinion on the Unified State Exam (EGE)?
It’s a very stressful period for every student. On the one hand, it’s a fair way to enter university, but on the other hand, the pressure is sometimes too high.
5. What would you like to do after you graduate from school?
I plan to enter a university and get a degree. I’m thinking about something related to linguistics or social sciences, but I’m still exploring my options.

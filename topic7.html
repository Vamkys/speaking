TOPIC 7: SCHOOLING IN RUSSIA
00:00

Task 1. Reading aloud

The school system in Russia is designed to provide students with a broad and solid foundation in various academic disciplines. Primary education begins at the age of six or seven and lasts for four years, followed by middle and senior school. The curriculum includes a wide range of subjects, from mathematics and literature to foreign languages and computer science. One of the unique features of Russian schools is the emphasis on classical literature, which helps students develop critical thinking and cultural awareness. At the end of their studies, students must take the Unified State Exam to enter universities. Recent reforms have introduced digital technologies into classrooms, allowing for more interactive and personalized learning. Despite these changes, the traditional role of the teacher as a mentor remains highly respected in Russian society.

Task 3. Interview

Listen to the audio and answer the questions.

1. What is your favourite subject at school and why?
My favourite subjects are English and Social Studies. I enjoy learning languages because they open up new worlds, and Social Studies helps me understand how society works.
2. What do you usually do during your school breaks?
I usually chat with my friends or check my phone. Sometimes I use this time to prepare for the next lesson or have a quick snack.
3. Do you think the Russian school system prepares students for real life?
In some ways, yes, because it teaches us discipline and basic knowledge. However, I think we need more practical lessons, like how to manage money or taxes.
4. What is your opinion on the Unified State Exam (EGE)?
It’s a very stressful period for every student. On the one hand, it’s a fair way to enter university, but on the other hand, the pressure is sometimes too high.
5. What would you like to do after you graduate from school?
I plan to enter a university and get a degree. I’m thinking about something related to linguistics or social sciences, but I’m still exploring my options.

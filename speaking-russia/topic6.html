Task 1. Reading aloud

The history of space exploration changed forever on April 12, 1961, when Yuri Gagarin became the first human to travel into outer space. His spacecraft, Vostok 1, completed a single orbit around the Earth, lasting only 108 minutes. This historic mission proved that humans could survive in weightlessness and paved the way for future lunar missions and space stations. Gagarin’s courage and his famous smile made him an international hero and a symbol of scientific progress. Since then, Russia has continued to play a leading role in space research, developing reliable rockets and maintaining the International Space Station. Every year, Cosmonautics Day is celebrated to honor the scientists and pilots who made space travel possible. The legacy of these pioneers continues to inspire new generations to look at the stars and dream of new discoveries.

Task 3. Interview

Listen to the audio and answer the questions.

1. What do you know about the first man in space?
The first man in space was Yuri Gagarin. He made his famous flight in 1961, which was a huge breakthrough for all of humanity.
2. Are you interested in news about space exploration? Why?
Yes, I find it fascinating because space is still a big mystery. I like reading about the possibility of living on other planets in the future.
3. What Russian scientific discovery do you find the most useful?
I think the invention of the radio by Alexander Popov was crucial. It paved the way for modern communication, including television and mobile phones.
4. Why do you think the government spends so much money on space programs?
It’s because space research leads to new technologies that we use in everyday life, like satellite navigation. It’s also important for national prestige.
5. Would you like to go to space as a tourist one day?
To be honest, I’m not sure because it seems quite dangerous. However, if the technology becomes safer, it would be an incredible experience to see the Earth from above.

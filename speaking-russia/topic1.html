Task 1. Reading aloud

Russia is famous for its historical routes, and the Golden Ring is perhaps the most celebrated one. This ring of ancient cities north-east of Moscow offers a unique glimpse into the country's medieval past. Each city, like Suzdal or Vladimir, features stunning white-stone churches and golden-domed cathedrals that date back centuries. These locations are not just open-air museums; they are living parts of Russian history where traditional crafts are still practiced. Tourists can enjoy the quiet atmosphere of the countryside while admiring the intricate architecture. Visiting the Golden Ring is a perfect way to escape the busy life of the capital and learn about the roots of Russian culture. Today, these cities are protected as World Heritage sites, ensuring that their beauty and historical significance will be preserved for future generations of travelers from all over the world.

Task 3. Interview

Listen to the audio and answer the questions.

1. What region of Russia do you live in? What is it famous for?
Actually, I live in the Oryol region. It is famous for its beautiful landscapes and its connection to great Russian writers like Turgenev.
2. What are the most popular tourist destinations in your country?
The most popular places are definitely Moscow and Saint Petersburg because of their history. However, Lake Baikal and the Altai Mountains are also becoming very trendy for nature lovers.
3. What historical landmarks in your city or region would you recommend visiting?
I’d recommend visiting the local parks and museums dedicated to our history. They offer a great atmosphere where you can learn about how people lived in the past.
4. Why do you think some people prefer travelling abroad rather than within Russia?
I suppose they want to experience different cultures and climates. Also, sometimes flights to Europe or Asia can be cheaper than domestic flights to distant parts of Russia like Kamchatka.
5. What would you say to a friend who is planning a trip to Russia for the first time?
I would advise them to prepare for the weather and learn some basic Russian phrases. Also, I’d suggest visiting both big cities and small traditional towns to see the real Russia.

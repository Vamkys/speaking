Task 1. Reading aloud

Alexander Pushkin is widely considered the greatest Russian poet and the founder of modern Russian literature. Born in 1799, he revolutionized the Russian language by mixing the formal grammar of the time with the natural speech of the people. His works, including "Eugene Onegin" and numerous fairy tales, are studied by every schoolchild in the country. Pushkin’s influence is so great that his birthday is now celebrated as Russian Language Day. He had a remarkable ability to express deep human emotions through simple yet beautiful verses. Although his life was tragically short, he left behind a massive literary heritage that continues to inspire writers and artists worldwide. To Russians, Pushkin is more than just a writer; he is a symbol of national pride and cultural identity.

Task 3. Interview

Listen to the audio and answer the questions.

1. Who is the most famous person from your home region?
As I’ve mentioned, my region is linked to Ivan Turgenev. He was a world-famous writer whose books are still studied in schools today.
2. In your opinion, which Russian scientist or inventor has changed the world?
I believe it’s Dmitry Mendeleev. His Periodic Table is the foundation of modern chemistry, and without it, many scientific discoveries wouldn't have happened.
3. What Russian athletes or sports figures are you proud of?
I’m proud of our figure skaters because they always show incredible results. Their discipline and hard work are a great example for young people.
4. Do you think it is easy to be a famous person nowadays? Why?
I don't think so, because famous people are always under pressure from social media. Every mistake they make is discussed by everyone, so it must be very stressful.
5. What field would you like to become famous in, and why?
To be honest, I haven't decided yet, but I'm interested in linguistics and education. I’d like to be known for helping people learn languages effectively.
